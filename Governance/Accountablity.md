Accountability is not applied automatically by default after authentication. Accountability involves tracking and recording actions, and it is often achieved through explicit mechanisms such as auditing.

It can't be delegated: Accountability is generally associated with the individual or entity responsible for actions. It can't be easily delegated to another party because it involves attributing actions to the specific entity that performed them.

Is usually achieved through auditing: Accountability is often achieved through auditing, which involves monitoring and recording events. Auditing helps create a record of actions taken by users, contributing to accountability.

Is the basis for forensics: Accountability is indeed the basis for digital forensics. When an incident occurs, the accountability information gathered through auditing can be crucial for investigating and understanding what happened.
